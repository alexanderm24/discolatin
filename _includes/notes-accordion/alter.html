Flavia puella altera est.

A common pattern of Latin adjectives is -us/-r, -a, -um (2-1-2) in which the forms are presented in the masculine, feminine, and neuter.
  • Example: alter, altera, alterum
    (Abbreviated: alter, -a, -um)
  • alter → masculine
    altera → feminine
    alterum → neuter
In the example sentence above, altera is chosen to modify puella because -a is the feminine ending. Consider the alternative examples below:
  • Example 1: Masculine
  • alter puer ambulat.
  • Example 2: Neuter
  • alterum templum est.

Adjectives often come after the noun they modify. Watch out for adjectives that are predicate nominatives.
